Biology: Aeroponics & Hydroponics

We use soil-less farming techniques to grow plants efficiently. These techniques offer a way to grow plants with minimal water and nutrients.

  • What is Aeroponics? Growing plants in air or mist without using soil. The roots are suspended in the air and sprayed with nutrient-rich water mist.
  • What is Hydroponics? Growing plants using mineral nutrient solutions in water, without the use of soil.

Chemistry: Water Purification

We designed a model of a wastewater treatment plant using natural materials to purify contaminated water.

  • Materials Used: Gravel, activated charcoal, sand, cotton, PVC pipes, and chlorine.
  • Process: We pass dirty water through different filtration stages (gravel, charcoal, sand, etc.) to clean it.

Physics: Hydraulic Jack Bridge

The hydraulic jack bridge demonstrates how hydraulic force can lift and support structures. The syringe is used as a hydraulic jack and the motor controls the force to raise or lower the bridge.

  • Materials: Syringes, cardboard, PVC pipes.
  • How it Works: The syringe acts as a hydraulic jack to lift the bridge when required. Hydraulics help us move and support heavy loads efficiently.

Computer Science: Smart Village Technology

We developed a website for the smart village, which automates various systems like energy management, water purification, and agricultural monitoring.

  • Technology Used: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, and Node.js.
  • Features: The website provides real-time data, interactive maps, and automated control over various systems like water and energy usage.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  • Q: What is aeroponics?
  • A: Aeroponics is the method of growing plants without soil, using mist or air to deliver nutrients to the roots.
  • Q: How does the hydraulic jack bridge work?
  • A: The hydraulic jack uses a syringe to lift a bridge, demonstrating hydraulic force and pressure.
